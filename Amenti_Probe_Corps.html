The Probe Corps

A tactical unit of diagnostics that patrols the fleet — cross-examining every claim against the living system, mostly unnoticed. This is their doctrine, their roster, and their stations. When you ask "where are the probes?", the answer is here.

AN INSTRUMENT SWORN TO ACCUSE ITSELF
BEFORE IT WILL LIE TO ITS CAPTAIN.

That is the whole doctrine. Everything below is a consequence of it. The three rules were not designed — each was written after a probe lied, and each is the instrument turning on its own failure rather than defending it. The warning most often quoted — a probe that reports red without looking is worse than no probe, a lie with a light on it — describes what happens when the founding idea is abandoned. It is the symptom, not the principle.

The Doctrine — three rules, each born from a lie

The doctrine is not theory. Three earlier probes lied, and each lie became a law. This is the hard-won core, taken from the head of amenti-probe.js:

Rule 1 · Test with REAL DATA

Use real prose, never 'x'.repeat(). A probe once tested the 1,100-char cap with 1,100 literal x's; the voice model couldn't speak that, returned 500, and the probe screamed "read-aloud is BROKEN — REVERT."

Garbage in, garbage verdict. Test with what the world actually sends.

Rule 2 · ATTRIBUTE, never infer

Read the response body. Name the actual cause. A probe once turned every non-200 into "the cap refused it" — so a 400 caused by the probe's own malformed request was reported as the wall breaking conversations at 10 exchanges.

It was terrifying and it was false. Read the body; name the real cause.

Rule 3 · The BODY always arrives — prefer it

Never let a missing signal become a false verdict. A probe once trusted a header the browser wasn't allowed to read, and reported the wall's every success as a failure.

A missing signal is not a red light. The body always arrives — read that.

This is the same doctrine as the Glass Gate — the one stamped into the engine room and the terminal: show what is real or show the empty box; never a comforting lie. The probes are that doctrine, armed and patrolling.

Where they live — the stations

The Instrument (root): amenti-probe.js — shipped on every surface of the main repo. Costs nothing until called. Append ?probe=1 to any URL for an instant state read.

The Corps (probes/ folder): probe.js through probe18.js, plus the fleet probes probe-watches.mjs and probe-ordnance.mjs. Each guards one claim.

The patrol (unnoticed): .github/workflows/patrol.yml runs the probes automatically in CI — they walk the fleet on their own, cross-examining the world against the manifest, and only speak up when something has gone blind. This is why they help "unnoticed."

In the panes: the fleet's watch.html (THE WATCH) and ordnance.html (ORDNANCE BAY) are the human-facing views of what probe-watches and probe-ordnance report.

The Corps turned on itself

A doctrine of self-accusation is worthless if it is only ever pointed outward. Three failures found inside the Corps, all in a single evening:

  • A probe certified a failure. Probe 19 Rev A tested whether the batch box held nine lines. It did. The probe reported eight passes while the splitter beside it was broken and every batch collapsed into one figure.
  • A probe asserted an unverified cause. On a missing panel it declared "this is the old console" — a conclusion, not an observation. A script error fits the same evidence.
  • A probe reported red where it was merely blind. Unable to find an expression in the page source, it returned FAIL. Not finding something means the instrument could not see, which is the third rule almost verbatim.
The corps is not exempt from the corps. A probe is delivered only when it has been accused, and the accusation reported — not when it is asked for.
