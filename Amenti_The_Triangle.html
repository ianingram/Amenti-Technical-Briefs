How the Voice Is Made

Four powers — Git, the Worker, Gemini, R2 — and why the shape of their arrangement is the whole design.

Everything the hall speaks passes through one arrangement of four parts. It is worth drawing that arrangement many ways, because each view reveals something the others hide: the mediation, the lifecycle, the economy, the doctrine, the two paths, and the roles. Six triangles, one truth — no single power can command the hall alone.

1 · The Hub

Begin with the shape itself. Three powers at the corners — Git the truth, Gemini the generator, R2 the memory — and at the center, the Worker, touching all three while holding nothing. The corners never speak to each other. Every exchange is brokered through the middle.

Figure 1 — The HubThe Worker mediates all three powers. It holds no data and generates nothing — it only brokers. That emptiness at the center is the separation made physical.

2 · The Lifecycle

Now watch a single voice travel the triangle. The truth is requested, the memory is asked first, and only on a miss does the expensive generator wake. The path favors rest: most requests never reach Gemini at all.

Figure 2 — The LifecycleA request asks memory before it asks the generator. The cache key decides which path it takes.

3 · The Economy

The same triangle, weighed by cost. Git is free. R2 is nearly free. Gemini is the only corner that charges — and the Worker's entire job is to touch it as rarely as possible. The cache key is not a technical detail; it is the economy.

Figure 3 — The EconomyThe expensive corner is touched least. Spend R2 always, spend Gemini rarely — the whole cost model in one shape.

4 · The Doctrine

Here is why the triangle matters beyond mechanics. Each corner is defined by what it cannot do. Git holds the word but cannot speak it. Gemini makes the voice but knows no truth and keeps nothing. R2 remembers every voice but authors none. None is whole. The hall speaks only when all three cooperate — and so no single captured corner can command it.

Figure 4 — The DoctrineEach power is incomplete by design. Divide the powers, and no captured corner can seize the hall.

5 · The Two Paths

Zoom into the decision at the heart of every request: hit or miss. The hit returns instantly, for nothing. The miss pays once, then becomes a hit forever. The archive only grows cheaper with use.

Figure 5 — Hit and MissThe miss pays once and joins the memory. Every voice made is a voice never paid for again.

6 · The Three Roles

Finally, the plainest view — each power named by its limit. Read down each column and the separation names itself: three parts, each strong in one thing, each helpless in the others, each depending on the whole.

Figure 6 — The Three RolesDefine each by what it cannot do, and the doctrine writes itself.

Six drawings, one architecture. The triangle is not a diagram of the system — it is the system's argument: that power divided among cooperating, incomplete parts is stronger, cheaper, and safer than power gathered in one place. The hall runs on that shape.
