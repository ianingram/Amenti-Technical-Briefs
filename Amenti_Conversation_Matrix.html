01The Two Faculties

The engine rests on a single separation, and everything else follows from it. Cognition and articulation are different faculties, and they are never braided into one pipeline.

The library thinks with one model and speaks with another. Claude composes the reply — the grounded, in-character thought. Gemini renders that text to a voice. Between them runs a single Cloudflare Worker, the nervous system, carrying signals but never merging the organs. This is not an implementation detail; it is the load-bearing wall. It means the voice can improve without touching the mind, and the mind without retuning the voice — and it means no single vendor owns the whole stack.

A MIND. A VOICE. ONE NERVOUS SYSTEM.
DO NOT BRAID THEM.

02The Matrix of Speech

The diagram below is the whole system on one board. The listening ear stands at the left; the voice parameters at the right; they face each other across a channel, and down the middle runs the seam that divides cognition from articulation. Read it as a single turn, in three beats: the room comes in on the left, the thought forms on the seam, the answer goes out on the right.

Each side carries its own parameters, and they do not cross the seam. The ear's world is a live audio stream: a microphone track, a running level, an echo floor, a silence timeout — and, at the last, a transcript, which is the only thing it hands inward. The voice's world is the cache key: a base timbre, a style string of register and accent, a measure boundary, a rate. The two never share a variable. What passes between them is text, and only text.

03The Flood, and the Metered Counsel

The Terminal's original speaker posted an entire reply to the voice worker in a single request and waited for all of it before a sound was heard. For a long answer that is thirty, forty, forty-six seconds of silence. This is the flood: fuel dumped into the cylinder all at once.

The cure is metering — cutting the reply into measures, rendering the first quickly, and speaking it while the rest are still being made. That engine existed, fully built and tested, switched off, with no caller. Connecting it, and cutting the opening measure short, moved first sound from the forties into single digits.

The curve was measured, not assumed — the inherited claim that latency was fixed regardless of chunk size was drawn from a monolith that never chunked. The floor of 7.5 seconds is real and irreducible per render; the win is in reaching it sooner with a short first measure.

04The Boundary — Character and Counsel

The figures answer in two registers. Character is Caesar being Caesar. Counsel is a therapeutic mode with a deliberate doctrine — statements over questions, a gentle arrest, a refusal to be triumphant. The doctrine is excellent for counsel and corrosive everywhere else, and it was being appended to both registers.

So the character prompt contradicted itself every turn, and it broke its own frame by naming the figure "a fictional device." The fix was a boundary: the counsel doctrine now stops at the counsel. Character mode was restored to an in-world frame — a visitor from a future age, a life inhabited rather than a dossier recited — and given a dial the seeker controls, and a lean/full choice to be settled by ear rather than by argument.

05The Cache Key, and the Ward that Guards It

Every rendered measure is stored under a key:

audioKey = sha256( TTS_MODEL + voice + STYLE + TEXT )

Because TEXT is a single measure, the chunk boundaries are the cache namespace. Move one byte of the model string, the style, or the chunker, and every clip in the archive is orphaned — silently — and must be re-rendered at cost. The recitation archive is weeks of audio and the entire cost story of the business.

The Worker that holds this key lives in Cloudflare, outside version control: no diff, no history, no rollback. It cannot be verified by reading it. So it is verified by speaking a known thing at it. A frozen 933-character passage is fired through both live profiles every six hours; six cache hits mean the key has not moved. A miss is not documentation drift — it is the archive forking, live, and the pattern of which measures miss is the diagnosis. The ward aborts after a single render, so a drifting archive can never become a runaway bill.

THE WORKER CANNOT BE READ. SO IT IS ASKED.
SIX HITS = THE KEY HELD. ONE MISS = THE ARCHIVE FORKED.

06The Gateway — Whose Turn It Is

The microphone is the one untrusted input in the system, and its state must be legible at a glance. The gateway draws it as a full-bleed wireframe pyramid — the interface the whole product already is. A pulse rising from the base is the seeker speaking; the lit capstone with nothing flowing is the mind at work, which gives the render floor a face instead of dead air; a pulse descending from the capstone is the library answering. The rise rides the real audio level, so it moves because a voice is present, not because a flag was set. A microphone that is live while nothing asked for it turns the gate red, and the gate will not close.

07The Hall — the Summon

Every conversational move in the doctrine points at the seeker — reflect, catch, observe. One move points the other way, at the library itself. A figure may name another who lived the same wound and send the visitor across the hall to them: Caesar, hearing of a father who would not bend, sends the seeker to Peter the Great, who buried his son for the state.

The name is validated against the roster, never against the model's memory — the moat of the whole product is verified grounding, so a summon that invents a person is a shot at that moat. Exact match only; an unknown name opens no door and the prose still reads. This is the flywheel: the hall recommends itself, in character, at the moment of greatest relevance.

08The Open Door

The last finding was a security hole, and it was found by discipline rather than insight. A policy file suggested the subscriber table's write access was unguarded; rather than assert it, the watch was taught to knock — a single non-destructive request against a row that cannot exist. The door opened. The public key shipped in every page could have rewritten every subscriber's address.

The read-watch had been green for months, because it proved the key could not read the table and no one had asked whether it could write. The fix removed write access entirely and replaced it with one narrow, single-purpose function. The lesson is the fleet's oldest.

A GREEN TICK PROVES ONE DOOR SHUT. IT SAYS NOTHING OF THE DOOR BESIDE IT.
PROBE FIRST. NEVER GUESS.
