This brief records what was built, what broke, and what the breakages taught.

I · What the surface was

At the start, everything lived in one place. The amenti-mint Worker held the quiz content, the answer keys, the scoring logic and the ledger writes in a single 66 KB file. Adding a quiz meant editing and redeploying the one piece of infrastructure that moves money. The arena showed six hand-typed cards in front of that library, and the two lists had already drifted — a card claimed "5 QUESTIONS" for a quiz that held four, and gave Lincoln an era his own quiz contradicted.

Worse, the count was wrong in the documents. Five briefs stated the library held eight quizzes. It held thirteen. The number had been carried forward from a summary and never checked against the source.

A DOCUMENT THAT IS NEVER CHECKED AGAINST THE SHIP
BECOMES A CONFIDENT DESCRIPTION OF SOMETHING THAT NO LONGER EXISTS.

II · The migration

The first structural change moved the quiz library out of the money Worker and into a topics table. It was done in four steps, deliberately, because the Worker that scores answers also writes to the ledger.

Figure 1 · before and after. The library left the Worker; the keys did not.

The order mattered more than the change. Copy first, verify the copy, then delete. A migration route was pasted into the live Worker so that it copied its own quizzes into the table — nothing retyped, nothing mistranscribed. Only once the count matched on both sides was the constant removed.

StepWhat it didVerified by
1Create the topics table, RLS on, no policies0 rows, no error

The proof the migration had truly happened came from an unexpected place: the JSON key order changed. Postgres jsonb sorts object keys by length then alphabetically, so figure, domain, motif… came back as era, motif, domain…. Code cannot do that. The data had made a round trip through the database.

The result is the point: adding a quiz is now one row insert. Four hours later, nine quizzes were added without Cloudflare being opened at all.

III · The anatomy of a quiz

The format was already in production and did not need designing — it needed writing down. Each quiz is a verified passage and six questions across three cognitive tiers, ending in the pair that feeds the court.

Figure 2 · the invariant. Every quiz, without exception.

Two rules emerged from writing them by hand that no document had captured:

The evaluate pair is not always champion-and-tyrant. For a ruler it is great deeds against tyranny. For a reformer it is the inspiring myth against the erased reality — Helen Keller's pump against the socialist the story forgets. For an explorer it is the robbed pioneer against the idea that "discovery" erases the people already there. Forcing one frame on every figure produces false quizzes.

The pans are cases, not questions. Each must begin "Make the strongest case that…" The court scores the quality of an argument, and a yes/no question cannot elicit one. This rule was learned when the engine returned "Was Einstein's 1905 work humanity's greatest achievement?" — structurally valid, and it breaks the entire mechanic.

IV · The Quizzard

The bottleneck was never the format. It was that every quiz came out of a conversation. The Quizzard is a Cloudflare Worker that takes one name and returns a validated quiz or an honest refusal.

Figure 3 · six gates and a halt rail. Every gate can refuse.

The load-bearing detail is that the model is called with the web search tool armed. A model writing from memory is precisely the fabrication risk the doctrine exists to prevent; search is what makes verification real rather than decorative.

The validator is a parser, not a review

Every returned quiz is checked in the Worker against the invariant — never by the model, never by a human first. Tested both directions before it was trusted: it accepts all five hand-written quizzes, and rejects nine deliberate corruptions including a dropped question, an out-of-range answer index, a tampered rubric and a third timed question.

Normalise constants, validate content

Three fields carry no information — the timed tiers, the philosophical word limit, the rubric. They are identical in every quiz ever written. Requiring a model to recall a constant, and discarding a researched quiz when it forgets, is friction with no benefit. The Worker supplies those three and nothing else: never a fact, never an option, never an answer index. Everything it fills is reported.

A HALT IS A SUCCESS, NOT A FAILURE.
THE ENGINE THAT CANNOT SAY NO WILL EVENTUALLY SAY SOMETHING FALSE.

V · The Bridge

A Worker with a secret-guarded POST route is unreachable from a browser address bar. The bridge is the console that drives it — and it is the only place the engine's work becomes visible: searches run, tokens spent, sources retrieved, and the raw response one click away.

It was first delivered as a file to download. That was a mistake, and it cost four rounds. A stale copy had no batch panel, so nine names pasted into a single-line field arrived as one sixteen-word figure. The Worker now serves its own console at its root, with no-store, so the tool and the engine cannot drift apart.

The bug beneath the bug

Even after the batch panel existed, every paste still collapsed. The cause was in the embedding: escaping every backslash turned split('\n') into split('\\n') — a literal backslash-n that never matches. The same escaping mangled ten unicode symbol escapes. The bridge contained no backticks and no interpolation, so it never needed escaping at all.

Probe 19 had reported eight green passes throughout, because it tested whether the textarea held nine lines. It did. The fault was one step later, in the splitter.

A PROBE THAT CHECKS THE COMPONENT NEXT TO THE BROKEN ONE
IS WORSE THAN NO PROBE — IT CERTIFIES THE FAILURE.

VI · The card

A card is not a name. Four artifacts, in four places, and three of four is a gap wearing a border.

Figure 4 · the four artifacts and where each lives.

The arena's cards were hand-written while the library was data — two lists maintained separately, already disagreeing. They are now generated from the live library: fetch /quiz/topics, build a card per quiz, attach the click handlers, then paint the portraits from the art library and the stat bars from the codex. Six cards became thirty-two, and drift became impossible.

Three failures worth recording

A card with nothing behind it served a stranger's quiz. Clicking Lincoln opened Caesar, because data-topic || DEFAULT_TOPIC substituted silently. A card with no quiz now says so and offers a picker of what is ready.

Ten characters were written, pasted correctly, and did not appear. AMENTI_CHARS was declared three separate times in Page1 — once each for the codex, timeline and terminal blocks. The last to run overwrote the others. AMENTI_ERAS had the same fault. Both are now declared once, and the page shed 62 KB.

The portraits were never six. A first attempt read .char-slide .char-art — the six hero-carousel slides — and concluded six figures were drawn. The real library was window.AMENTI_SVG: twenty-one hand-built portraits keyed by codex key. The correction doubled the faces on the roster before a single new one was drawn.

IF A LIST IS DECLARED TWICE, ONE OF THEM IS A LIE WAITING TO BE TOLD.

VII · The art migration

Twelve portraits were drawn to close the gap — Douglass, Keller, Prometheus, Loki, Odysseus, Gilgamesh, Arthur, Lycurgus, Manlius, Rand, Leif and Ingram. Each is a hand-built SVG on the house 320×560 stage, matching the head geometry and shared defs() of the twenty-one that came before, with accent colours read from each figure's codex record rather than invented.

They were first delivered as blocks to paste into Page1. That was the wrong instruction and the captain said so: art belongs in files that grow, not pasted into a 1.6 MB page every time. They now live in amenti-art-2.js, which attaches to the library Page1 created — or creates it if it loads first, so load order cannot break it — and refreshes the roster once the art is in.

Each was drawn from its own stat line rather than a generic pose. Gilgamesh — 96 combat, 66 foresight — stands with his back to the walls the epic offers as his true monument. Rand, at 14 combat the smallest fighter in the set, carries a cigarette holder and a manuscript. Odysseus is roped to his own mast, because he wanted to hear the song and survive it, so he arranged both.

VIII · The Corps turned on itself

The Probe Corps exists to cross-examine claims against the living system. It was the one thing nothing was checking.

The scanner that catalogues the fleet excluded probes by name and never walked probes/ at all: nineteen files on disk, one in the manifest, and a pane claiming twenty in rows typed by hand. It could have claimed any number.

Both ends are fixed. The scan now catalogues the folder and reads each probe's codename from its own header; the pane renders that manifest instead of a remembered list, and goes dark rather than guessing if it cannot be read.

X · What the failures taught

Nine faults were found and fixed. Every one was visible to anyone who looked, and none required insight.

Douglass filed as Early Modernreading the output
The last one is the instructive one. "Early Modern" is a legal value in the controlled vocabulary — the validator passed it. A vocabulary check catches invented words, not wrong choices among real ones. That class of error is exactly why quizzes are written as staged and promoted by hand.

XI · What remains

Ingram has no quiz, and that is the correct gap: a living founder is precisely the case the doctrine says not to put on trial.

Canon mode is specified and unarmed. Roughly a hundred and fifty figures — the mythic tier, the legendary founders, the scriptures — sit behind it. The engine refuses mode: canon until the interpretive frames for religious figures are written, which is the single place in the fleet where autonomy is withheld by design.

The patrol is unbuilt. The engine runs when a button is pressed. The queue, the staging table and the schedule that would let it work down the roster unattended are drawn but not made.

EVERY STAGE THAT STILL REQUIRES HIM IS A STAGE NOT YET FINISHED.
